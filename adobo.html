Adobo

Ingredients:

* 2 lbs (about 1 kg) chicken (or pork, or a combination of both), cut into pieces
* 1/2 cup soy sauce
* 1/2 cup vinegar (white or cane vinegar)
* 1 cup water
* 1 onion, sliced
* 4-6 cloves garlic, minced
* 2-3 bay leaves
* 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns
* 2 tablespoons cooking oil (for sautéing)

Instructions:

1. In a large bowl, combine the chicken pieces, soy sauce, minced garlic, and bay leaves. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes, or preferably overnight in the refrigerator for more flavor.
2. Heat the cooking oil in a large pot or skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they are translucent.
3. Remove the chicken from the marinade (reserve the marinade) and add it to the pot. Brown the chicken on all sides for about 5-7 minutes.
4. Pour in the reserved marinade, vinegar, and water. Add the whole black peppercorns and the green chili peppers if using. Stir to combine.
5. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the chicken is tender and the sauce has thickened. Stir occasionally.
6. Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning if needed. If you prefer a slightly sweeter adobo, you can add brown sugar at this point.
7. Once the chicken is cooked and the sauce is thick, remove it from heat. Serve hot with steamed white rice, and optionally garnish with chopped green onions or parsley. You can also add hard-boiled eggs for extra flavor.
