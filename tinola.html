Tinola

Ingredients:

* 2 lbs (about 1 kg) chicken, cut into pieces (bone-in pieces are preferred for flavor)
* 1 tablespoon cooking oil
* 1 onion, sliced
* 4-6 cloves garlic, minced
* 1-2 inches fresh ginger, sliced or julienned
* 6 cups water (or chicken broth for more flavor)
* 1 medium green papaya (or chayote), peeled and cut into wedges
* 2 cups fresh spinach or chili leaves (or a combination of both)
* 2-3 pieces green chili peppers (optional, for heat)

Instructions:

1. In a large pot, heat the cooking oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté until they become translucent.
2. Add the minced garlic and sliced ginger, and sauté for an additional 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
3. Add the chicken pieces to the pot and cook until they are browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes.
4. Pour in the water (or chicken broth) and add fish sauce if using. Bring the mixture to a boil.
5. Once boiling, reduce the heat to low and cover the pot. Let it simmer for about 30 minutes or until the chicken is cooked through and tender.
6. Add the green papaya (or chayote) to the pot and simmer for another 5-10 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
7. If using, add the green chili peppers during the last few minutes of cooking.
8. Stir in the fresh spinach or chili leaves, and cook for another 1-2 minutes until the greens are wilted.
9. Taste the soup and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if needed.
10. Serve hot with steamed rice, and offer lemon or lime wedges on the side for added flavor.
